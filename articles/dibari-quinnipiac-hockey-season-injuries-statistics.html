Success Through Setbacks

Quinnipiac Men's Ice Hockey Teams Ability to Overcome The Loss of Injured Players This Season

Izzy DiBari

April 28, 2026

The Quinnipiac Men’s Hockey team has had a strong year, with a final record of 27-10-3, after facing North Dakota in the NCAA regionals.

Even with a strong showing for the 2025-26 season, the Bobcats faced several setbacks, including star players injured and out of the game. Some sat out for a handful of games, while others were out for the entirety of the season.

In December, senior assistant captain Jeremy Wilmer left a game early due to an ACL tear– a season ending injury. Shortly after Wilmer’s injury, the team’s other senior assistant captain, Charlie Leddy, faced an injury to his meniscus and has only recently come back to the ice to play with his team. Other key players were injured during the period of both Leddy and Wilmer being out, including Bruins prospect Chris Pelosi and sophomore Tyler Borgula, who scored 12 goals in his freshman season.

Despite having these four key players out for a big part of the season, the rest of the roster put up big numbers for the team. Freshman Flames prospect Ethan Wyttenbach and sophomore Bruins prospect Elliot Groenwold are amongst those players, but there are others who contributed greatly to the success of the team. Those players included defensemen Will Gilson and Graham Sward, along with forward Markus Vidicek and Antonin Verrault. Gilson was on the ice for 58 of the Bobcat goals, with Sward for 51, Vidicek for 61 and Verrault for 55.

During this injury period, a few players really stood out when viewing their performance based on their assists and goals scored. Looking at the team as a whole, some games had very impressive end results. There was their 9-1 win against Harvard, their 7-2 win against Union (after a 0-4 loss last time they faced them), and three-five goal games. In this winning streak, Gilson scored 3 goals and 3 assists, Groenwold had 2 goals and 5 assists, Wyttenbach with 5 goals and 5 assists, and Verrault with 3 goals and 7 assists.

Wyttenbach and Groenwold are two players that fans expected a lot out of and are definitely receiving such strong statistics. As a defensemen, Groenwold had scored 6 goals and had 13 assists this season. Groenwold was named the ECAC Hockey best defensive defensemen this season. As a freshman, Wyttenbach had scored 21 goals and had 32 assists, awarding him the 2026 ECAC Rookie of the Year, as well as a top 10 finalist for the Hobey Baker Award.

Wyttenbach has proven to be a star forward for the Bobcats, and Groenwold as a defensemen as they have both put up great numbers this season, but other players have also stepped up while the other players were hurt. For instance, sophomore defenseman Braden Blace went from being scratched some games his freshman year, to being in the starting lineup the second half of his sophomore season. The first half of this season he’d put up a point here and there, but come January, he had a streak of at at least one point for five games straight– one of those games having three points.

Another player that had shown significant improvement is senior forward Anthony Cipollone, who had put up only 2 points in the first half of the season, to 8 points in the second half. This is a solid jump from his junior season, where he only put up 3 points total.

Overall, there had been a lot of growth and strong showings from the Bobcats this season, even despite their setbacks. Players with big names continued to thrive, and other players were able to rise to the occasion and prove themselves while their teammates were hurt. Despite it all, the team came together and shined this season, as they overcame injuries and setbacks to win the ECAC championship for the sixth consecutive year.
