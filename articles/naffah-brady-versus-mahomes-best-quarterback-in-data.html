Tom Brady vs Patrick Mahomes: Who is truly the GOAT?

A statistical breakdown of two of the top quarterbacks in NFL history

Zach Naffah

April 28, 2026

The quarterback is often referred to as the most valuable position in all of sports. While many players have gained notoriety in the position, most would say that Tom Brady is the greatest to ever step on the field. Brady played 23 seasons in the NFL and is the career record holder for various metrics after his retirement in 2023 at the age of 45. He became the starter in 2001 (his second season in the NFL) for the New England Patriots before spending the last three years of his career with the Tampa Bay Buccaneers. While Brady compiled gaudy numbers over his lengthy career, there is a younger challenger to his “throne” as the greatest of all time.

Patrick Mahomes’ career began similarly to Brady’s. The Kansas City Chiefs superstar started on the bench for a season just like Brady before he exploded on the scene in 2018. Since then, Mahomes has been at or near the top in award voting and statistical categories year in and year out before his torn ACL in 2025. Through the first seven years as starting quarterbacks, both players won three Super Bowls, with Mahomes having an edge on Brady in a one-to-one comparison during similar points of their careers.

Is it possible to definitively say which quarterback is truly the greatest? How does greatness correlate with efficiency? To answer these questions and more, it’s worth breaking their careers down into multiple aspects.

The first part is analyzing the first seven years of their careers as starters to prove which one had a better start to their career. The numbers show Mahomes with higher stats, both in total output and on a per game basis.

It is also worth comparing their numbers to the rest of the league during those periods to show each quarterback’s output relative to the rest of the NFL at the time. The NFL as a whole has seen an increase in passing frequency, but both players’ numbers are still stellar.

Another aspect is comparing the supporting casts of each quarterback. While there is not one perfect way to do this, the easiest is by highlighting the number of Pro Bowl players each of them had during their first seven years as starters. This shows Mahomes having a significantly stronger team on the offensive side, whereas Brady didn’t have many star players on offense to help contribute to his statistical output over his first seven years.

Furthermore, while greatness is not truly quantifiable, contrasting how each has performed in the playoffs is significant. There is more pressure to step up and lead your team to victory, and both quarterbacks have similar per-game metrics, such as passing yards and touchdowns.

Overall, the numbers show Mahomes having a stronger start to a career from a statistical standpoint, and while he comes from a more pass-happy era, he stands out amongst his peers. On the other hand, Brady’s longevity and improvement over time make it difficult for Mahomes to potentially catch him in pure counting numbers, while his postseason success is also significant.
