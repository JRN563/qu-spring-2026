Where is the Magic of Brazil – the Country of Soccer?

For many years Brazil was known as the country of soccer for their many titles and star players such as Pelé, Ronaldinho, Kaká and Neymar. But in recent years, the national team has been struggling to win yet another World Cup

Giulia Zoli Ferreira

April 28, 2026

It was supposed to be a regular day – unless you were Brazilian. If you ask any Brazilian what they were doing on July 8th, 2014, they will remember. Maybe they won’t recall the exact date, but you can also ask “what were you doing the day Germany scored seven goals against Brazil?” and they will know what you are talking about.

2014 was supposed to be a big year for Brazilian soccer, and therefore for every Brazilian. The country hosted the FIFA Men’s World Cup. The national team looked promising with Neymar, Marcelo, Thiago Silva, and more. Brazil was battling to achieve what no other national team has achieved: six World Cup titles. Nobody would guess what happened in the next 90 minutes, not even the next 30 minutes when Germany had already scored four goals. The end result was Germany 7 - 1 Brazil. After that, there was a whole commotion in the community and a question that hasn't been answered until this day: what happened to the Brazilian national team? Why are they not as successful as they once were?

Stable at the FIFA Ranking, But What About Now?

Brazil is the only team to participate in all editions of the World Cup since its first run in 1930, but the governing body of FIFA kept a ranking beginning in 1992 that is updated after each official match is played. The South American country has an impressive record in the ranking. Now, two months before the 2026 edition of the World Cup and by the time this article was written , they are sitting at 6th place in a 210 team rank. For a while they were the number one team.

Brazil’s worst ranking was in 2013, a year before the 2014 World Cup, at 22nd place. Maybe that was a warning

Are They Still Productive?

After that traumatic game against Germany, some people wondered – how did one team score seven goals against the other, all in one game? Has Brazil been scoring less goals than before? The following World Cup results haven’t answered that question, unfortunately.

Among other winning teams this millennium, Brazil is the most regular team when it comes to average goals per World Cup. With the exception of 2002, when they scored 18 goals, the green and yellow team scored between 11 and 8 goals. Other teams have not shown the same consistency as them.

Does Playing in the Strongest Leagues Matter?

No doubt that the Premier League in England and La Liga in Spain are the strongest and most competitive leagues in the world. But does it have an effect - positive or negative - in the national team’s results? Does having more players playing in the strongest leagues automatically make you a good team?

It is interesting to notice that in the 2002 winning team, eleven players were in the Brasileirão, the domestic league in Brazil, while in the subsequent editions of the World Cup we see more and more players that were playing abroad. Some people say that the national team has lost the true Brazilian spirit and identity, and that may be a reflection on players adopting more of a European style of playing.

So...What's the Deal?

Even though Brazil has had regular average goals in recent World Cups, the team never really recovered from its drop from 2011 in FIFA’s ranking. Has Brazilian soccer lost its magic along the way? In a few months, the 2026 World Cup will be held throughout North America, and Brazil will have the chance to prove if they are still at the top of the game or if their fans will have to stick with the good old memories from now on.
